Basic Information:

I am an associate professor at the University of Southern California. I received my Ph.D. from New York University in 2011, after receiving my B.A. from Western Washington University and spending 2 years of graduate school at Rutgers University.

My primary areas of research are Metaphysics and Philosophy of Religion. Within Metaphysics, I am particularly interested in Mereology, Ontology, Time, Location, and Modality. I have also done research in Philosophical Linguistics and Aesthetics. For more information on what I'm working on, you can visit my Research Page.
