Course Overview

We will be looking at a variety of contemporary debates in Metaphysics, addressing questions such as:  What sorts of entities are people?  How do entities persist?  Do past and future times exist?  How is change possible?  What is freedom, and do any of us have it?  And what is causation?  We will also briefly look at Epistemology, focusing questions like:  what is the nature of justification, and how do beliefs come to have it?  And if belief is involuntary, how can we have epistemic duties?

Requirements

There will be a midterm exam, a final exam, a short (15 minute) presentation, and pop-quizzes throughout the term. The pop-quizzes cannot be made up if you miss class. However, you can turn in a quiz paper if you know ahead of time that you're going to miss class and get permission in person first. As for the exams: a week before each exam, about 5 or 6 essay questions will be posted online. The day of the exam, I will choose at least one of these.

The midterm and final will each be worth 30% of the final grade, the presentation will be worth 10%. The pop-quizzes together are worth 30% of the final grade.

Text

Our textbook will be Metaphysics: The Big Questions, edited by Peter van Inwagen and Dean Zimmerman. There will be additional readings made available to you during the course.
